Phrasal Verbs 41-60

  • Keep Up - maintain, continue
    • Examples:
      • She tries to keep up with the latest trends.
      • He kept up his exercise routine even on vacation.
      • They kept up the tradition every year.
    • Questions:
      • How do you keep up with changes in your industry?
      • What’s something you try to keep up with regularly?
      • Have you ever struggled to keep up with something? What was it?


  • Let Down - disappoint
    • Examples:
      • She felt let down by her friend's behavior.
      • He didn’t want to let his parents down.
      • They were let down by the service at the restaurant.
    • Questions:
      • Have you ever felt let down by someone? What happened?
      • How do you handle it when you let someone down?
      • What’s the most significant letdown you’ve experienced?


  • Take Off - remove, leave the ground
    • Examples:
      • She took off her shoes after a long day.
      • The plane took off at 6 PM.
      • He took off his jacket because it was hot.
    • Questions:
      • How do you feel during a plane’s take-off?
      • What’s the first thing you take off when you get home?
      • Have you ever taken off on a spontaneous trip? Where did you go?


  • Look After - take care of
    • Examples:
      • She looks after her younger siblings.
      • He asked his neighbor to look after the house while he was away.
      • They look after their pets with great care.
    • Questions:
      • How do you look after yourself when feeling stressed?
      • Have you ever had to look after someone else’s pet? What was it like?
      • What’s the most challenging thing about looking after children?


  • Look For - search
    • Examples:
      • She’s looking for her keys.
      • He’s looking for a new job.
      • They’re looking for volunteers for the event.
    • Questions:
      • What’s something you often find yourself looking for?
      • How do you feel when you can’t find something you’re looking for?
      • Have you ever helped someone look for something important? What was it?


  • Find Out - discover
    • Examples:
      • She found out the truth about the situation.
      • He found out that he had won the lottery.
      • They found out their flight was delayed.
    • Questions:
      • What was the last surprising thing you found out?
      • How do you usually react when you find out unexpected news?
      • Have you ever found out a secret? What did you do with the information?


  • Bring Out - highlight, produce
    • Examples:
      • She brought out the best in him.
      • He brought out a new line of clothing.
      • They brought out the cake for her birthday.
    • Questions:
      • Who or what brings out the best in you?
      • How do you feel when someone brings out your flaws?
      • Have you ever brought out a product or idea? How was it received?


  • Break Down - explain in detail
    • Examples:
      • She broke down the project into manageable tasks.
      • He broke down the complex topic for his students.
      • They broke down the steps to solve the problem.
    • Questions:
      • How do you break down complex tasks?
      • Have you ever had to break down a difficult concept for someone? How did it go?
      • What’s the most challenging thing to break down for others?


  • Call Back - return a phone call
    • Examples:
      • She called back after missing his call.
      • He asked her to call back later.
      • They called back the customer to resolve the issue.
    • Questions:
      • How do you feel about calling back missed calls?
      • Have you ever had to call back someone important? What was it about?
      • What’s your strategy for remembering to call people back?


  • Look Back - reflect on the past
    • Examples:
      • She looked back on her childhood with fondness.
      • He looked back and realized his mistakes.
      • They often look back on their travels.
    • Questions:
      • How often do you look back on your past experiences?
      • What’s something you enjoy looking back on?
      • Have you ever looked back and wished you had done something differently?


  • Look Out For - be alert to
    • Examples:
      • Look out for any suspicious behavior.
      • She always looks out for her friends.
      • They looked out for opportunities to invest.
    • Questions:
      • How do you look out for potential dangers?
      • Have you ever had someone look out for you? How did it feel?
      • What’s something you should look out for in your daily life?


  • Work Out - exercise, solve a problem
    • Examples:
      • She works out every morning.
      • They worked out the solution to the problem.
      • Things didn't work out, so we decided to break up.
    • Questions:
      • How often do you work out? What’s your routine?
      • Have you ever worked out a difficult problem? What was it?
      • How do you feel when something doesn’t work out as planned?


  • Put Away - store, save
    • Examples:
      • She put away the groceries.
      • He put away some money for his vacation.
      • They put away the toys after playing.
    • Questions:
      • How do you feel about putting away your belongings?
      • Do you have a routine for putting away items after use?
      • What’s something you always put away for future use?


  • Show Off - boast, display
    • Examples:
      • He likes to show off his new car.
      • She showed off her dancing skills at the party.
      • They show off their wealth by buying expensive clothes.
    • Questions:
      • How do you feel about people who show off?
      • Have you ever shown off something you’re proud of?
      • What’s the best way to handle someone who likes to show off?


  • Take Back - retract, return
    • Examples:
      • She took back her words after realizing they were hurtful.
      • He took back the faulty product to the store.
      • They took back the invitation when plans changed.
    • Questions:
      • Have you ever had to take back something you said? How did it go?
      • How do you handle taking back an item to a store?
      • What’s something you wish you could take back?


  • Break In - enter forcibly, interrupt
    • Examples:
      • Someone tried to break in last night.
      • She broke in on their conversation.
      • The new shoes need time to break in.
    • Questions:
      • How do you ensure your home is secure against break-ins?
      • Have you ever had to break in on a conversation? Why?
      • How do you break in new shoes or equipment?


  • Break Out - escape, develop suddenly
    • Examples:
      • The prisoners attempted to break out.
      • She broke out in a rash.
      • War broke out between the two countries.
    • Questions:
      • What would you do if a fire broke out in your building?
      • How do you handle it when you break out in a rash?
      • Have you ever broken out of a restrictive situation?


  • Come Across - find by chance
    • Examples:
      • She came across some old photos.
      • He came across as very friendly.
      • They came across a great restaurant in the city.
    • Questions:
      • What’s the most interesting thing you’ve come across recently?
      • How do you feel when you come across a long-lost item?
      • Have you ever come across someone unexpectedly?


  • Come Up With - think of
    • Examples:
      • She came up with a brilliant idea.
      • He came up with an excuse for being late.
      • They came up with a solution to the problem.
    • Questions:
      • What’s the best idea you’ve ever come up with?
      • How do you usually come up with solutions to problems?
      • Have you ever had to come up with an excuse on the spot?


  • Drop Out - leave, quit
    • Examples:
      • He dropped out of college.
      • She dropped out of the race due to injury.
      • They dropped out of the project halfway through.
    • Questions:
      • Have you ever dropped out of a course or activity? Why?
      • How do you feel about people who drop out of commitments?
      • What’s a time when you felt like dropping out but didn’t?
