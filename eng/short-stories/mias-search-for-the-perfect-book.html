Mia's Search for the Perfect Book

Mia loves to read, so today she decided to visit the library. She wants to find a popular book called Bun and the Shiny Red Ball. Mia enters the library. It is very quiet. She looks at the shelves, but she cannot find the book that she wants.

Mia walks to different sections of the library. She checks the fiction section, the mystery section, and even the science section, but the book is not there. She feels a little frustrated. Finally, Mia decides to ask the librarian for help.

The librarian smiles and says, "Let me check the computer." After a few seconds, the librarian says, "I found it! The book is on the top shelf in the corner in section A23." Mia thanks the librarian and goes to section A23. She reaches up, and there it is—Bun and the Shiny Red Ball! Mia is very happy.

Mia finds a comfortable chair in the library. She opens the book and begins to enjoy the story. But suddenly, she hears a noise. Someone has brought a puppy into the library! The puppy is barking and making a lot of noise. Mia thinks, "I should ask them to be quiet so I can read."

Mia gets up and walks to find the puppy and its owner. But when Mia sees the puppy, she smiles. The puppy is so cute! Mia can't help herself. Instead of asking for them to be quiet, she puts her book down and goes to play with the puppy. They have a lot of fun together. The end.

Vocabulary

  • Section - An area or part of something, such as a part of a library where certain books are kept.
    • The fiction section in the library is full of great books.
    • Mia went to the science section to look for her book.
    • The librarian told Mia to check the section for new arrivals.
  • Frustrated - Feeling annoyed or upset because you cannot do or find something.
    • Mia felt frustrated because she could not find the book.
    • When the book was not on the shelf, Mia got a little frustrated.
    • It is easy to feel frustrated when something is lost.
  • Shelf - A flat surface in a library or store where items like books are placed.
    • Mia looked at every shelf but could not see her book.
    • The book was on the top shelf, too high for Mia to reach.
    • The librarian helped Mia find the right shelf in the library.
  • Librarian - A person who works in a library and helps people find books and information.
    • The librarian helped Mia find her book.
    • Mia asked the librarian for help when she could not find the book.
    • The librarian checked the computer to find the book's location.
  • Bark - The sound a dog makes.
    • The puppy began to bark loudly in the library.
    • Mia heard the puppy bark and went to see it.
    • Dogs bark when they are excited or want attention.

Comprehension Questions

  1. What book was Mia looking for?
  2. Who did Mia ask for help?
  3. Where did the librarian find the book?
  4. What noise did Mia hear in the library?
  5. What did Mia do instead of reading?

Discussion Questions

  1. What is your favourite book, and why do you like it?
  2. Do you prefer reading physical books or e-books? Why?
  3. Do you have a favourite author? Who is it and what do they write?
  4. What was the last book you read? Did you enjoy it?
  5. Do you prefer fiction or non-fiction books? Why?
  6. Have you ever recommended a book to someone? What was it?
  7. Do you think it's important for people to read books? Why or why not?
  8. What is the first book you remember reading?
  9. Have you ever re-read a book? Why did you choose to read it again?
  10. Do you think libraries are important in communities? Why?
  11. What is your favourite place to read?
  12. Do you like to read before going to bed? Why or why not?
  13. Do you keep a list of books you want to read? What’s on your list?
  14. What do you think about audiobooks? Do you like listening to them?
  15. If you could write a book, what would it be about?
